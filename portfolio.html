Biostatistics in R


  • Probability Analysis of Genetic Diseases and Health Metrics

    • I explored various probability scenarios related to genetic diseases, antibody testing, obesity prevalence, and diabetes sampling distributions. By employing statistical methods such as Punnett squares, sensitivity and specificity calculations, and simulations, I derived insights into the likelihood of conditions like sickle cell anemia and the effectiveness of SARS-CoV-2 antibody tests. Additionally, I analyzed population data to understand the expected prevalence of obesity and diabetes in the U.S., highlighting the importance of statistical literacy in interpreting health-related metrics.


  • Exploring the Relationship Between BMI, Glycohemoglobin, and Diabetes Status

    • This project involved analyzing the NHANES glycohemoglobin dataset to explore the relationships between body mass index (BMI) and diabetes diagnosis. Through various visualizations, including histograms, density plots, and boxplots, the analysis highlighted key differences in BMI distributions based on diabetes status, sex, and race/ethnicity. The findings demonstrate the significant overlap in BMI among different diabetes categories while also revealing trends such as higher glycohemoglobin levels in individuals with diabetes compared to non-diabetics.
